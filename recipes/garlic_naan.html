Garlic Naan

Description

This homemade garlic naan recipe is cooked in a hot cast iron skillet rather than a tandoor oven, like restaurant naan. When cooked at the proper temperature, naan develops blistered bubbles with a lovely golden-black char. Reward yourself for a job well done by sopping the bread in copious amounts of curry.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup warm water, or more as needed
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups bread flour, or more as needed
  • ¼ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (Optional)

Directions

  1. Activate the yeast by combining water, sugar, and yeast; let it sit until foamy.
  2. Melt butter and sauté garlic, then remove from heat.
  3. Mix flour, yogurt, salt, garlic butter, and yeast mixture; knead into a smooth dough.
  4. Let the dough rise in a bowl drizzled with garlic butter until it doubles in size.
  5. Shape the dough into balls, proof them, and roll out into thin ovals.
  6. Heat a skillet and cook the naan until bubbles form, then flip and cook until charred.
  7. Brush naan with garlic butter and serve.
